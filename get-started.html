First session prep

Get started

A short checklist so your first lesson feels unhurried. Nothing complicated — most of it takes just a few minutes.

Checklist

You don’t need to be a tech expert. If you can browse the web and join a video call, you’re ready. All you need is a device and an internet connection.

Have a device ready

A computer or tablet works best — you’ll be watching my screen as we work. A smartphone will do, but a larger screen makes it easier to follow along.

Join the Microsoft Teams meeting link

I’ll email you a link before your session. Click it and choose “Continue on this browser” — that’s the easiest option. The Teams app also works if you prefer it.

Check microphone + speakers

Headphones are optional, but can reduce echo. We can test audio in the first 2 minutes.

Choose your AI tool (free)

I teach with ChatGPT, but you’re welcome to use Claude or Gemini — they work the same way. If you don’t have an account yet, signing up is free and takes a minute or two. You’ll find official links on this page.

Join a few minutes early

Joining a few minutes early gives us time to check your audio and get settled. The Chrome or Edge browser on a computer tends to work best.

Think about what you’d like to try

No preparation needed — but if something comes to mind, bring it along. A problem you’ve been wrestling with, a trip you’re planning, something you’ve been curious about. We’ll use real examples in class either way.

What to expect

Sessions run about 60 minutes. We start with a quick audio check, then jump in. You won’t be put on the spot — ask questions whenever they come up, or just follow along. You’ll leave with a few things you can repeat on your own.

Pricing: $35 per person (small group, 2–4) · $60 (one-on-one)

Joining your session

You’ll join class through Microsoft Teams. Most people just click the link I send and open it in the browser — no app needed. If you’d rather have a dedicated app, the download is here. Either way works.
